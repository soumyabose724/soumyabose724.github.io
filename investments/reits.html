REIT/InvIT distributions in India are split across three components with different tax treatment: the dividend portion (taxable, unless the SPV opted for the old tax regime), interest income (fully taxable at slab rate), and amortisation of SPV debt (treated as return of capital — reduces your cost basis and is tax-free until it exceeds it, then taxed as capital gains). Your broker's tax P&L statement usually itemises this split per distribution — reconcile it at ITR time rather than assuming the full payout is tax-free.
