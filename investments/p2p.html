P2P Carries Real Default Risk
The 9.6% actual return is already below the ~12% advertised/expected rate — the gap is defaults and delays, and this is normal for the category, not a platform malfunction. RBI caps individual exposure at ₹50 lakh per lender across all NBFC-P2P platforms and requires diversification, but there is no deposit insurance, no capital guarantee, and returns are not assured — unlike a bank FD. Treat P2P as a high-risk, high-yield sleeve (typically capped at 3–5% of net worth), not a fixed-income substitute. Defaulted amounts are rarely recovered in full even after collection efforts.
