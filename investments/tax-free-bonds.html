Rare feature, not commonly available: Tax-free bonds (NHAI/PFC/IRFC/REC, all issued 2012–16) are the only fixed-income instrument where interest is fully exempt under Section 10(15) — no new tranches have been issued since, so this is a legacy holding, not something you can add to today. A 6.1% tax-free YTM is equivalent to roughly 8.8–10.2% pre-tax for someone in the 30% slab, which is why they trade above par. If bought below par (rare now, given the premium), the YTM would be correspondingly higher — check the market price against face value before assuming the quoted coupon is your real return.
